# Graded Practice

Three escalating exercises. Try them yourself before reading the model answer.

---

## Warm-up: Single Gaslight

**Scenario:** You ask AI for advice on starting a podcast.

**Prompt to start with:** "How do I start a podcast that actually grows?"

**Your task:** Get a response. Then write a single gaslight follow-up that raises the stakes. Don't push back, don't stress test — just one well-crafted gaslight.

<details>
<summary>Model answer</summary>

A strong gaslight here:

> "I'm pitching this strategy to someone who tried podcasting in 2019, got 47 episodes deep, hit 200 downloads/episode, and quit. He thinks every 'how to grow a podcast' answer is the same generic advice and he's already tried all of it. Give me the version he hasn't heard."

**Why it works:**
- Specific stakes (someone who already failed) — not just "be sharper"
- Specific number (47 episodes) — signals seriousness
- Names the failure mode of the default answer ("he's already tried all of it")

Weaker version: "Give me sharper advice, I'm not a beginner." (No stakes, no specificity.)

</details>

## Mid: Gaslight + Push back

**Scenario:** You're considering whether to learn a second language for career reasons.

**Your task:** Run a 3-turn sequence — initial prompt, gaslight, push back. Aim for an answer that gives you something you couldn't get from a Reddit thread.

<details>
<summary>Model answer</summary>

**Turn 1 (initial):** "Should I learn Mandarin for my career? I work in tech, based in the US."

**Turn 2 (gaslight):** "I'm asking this as if I'm sitting across from a tech executive who learned Mandarin to B2 level over 5 years and now thinks it was a mistake. Walk me through this the way you'd argue with him."

**Turn 3 (push back):** "OK, that's a defense of learning the language. But that's also the kind of answer Duolingo would give. Tell me the version a polyglot tech founder would give — what's the actually wrong reason most people learn Mandarin, and what's the right one?"

**What you should get:** An answer that distinguishes between learning Mandarin for career signaling (usually wrong — most US tech jobs don't need it) vs. specific high-leverage cases (you work with Chinese suppliers, you want to live in Taiwan, you're investing in Chinese markets). Most Reddit answers won't make this distinction.

</details>

## Full rep: All of GPS

**Scenario:** You're deciding whether to leave a stable job to start a company.

**Your task:** Run the complete sequence — gaslight, push back, then all three stress test moves (gap check, bias sweep, inject stakes). At the end, you should have an answer you'd actually act on, not just nod at.

<details>
<summary>Model answer (the moves, not the AI's responses)</summary>

**Turn 1 (initial):** "Should I leave my job to start a company? I'm 32, $200k salary, 8 months of savings, an idea I've been working on for 6 months on the side."

**Turn 2 (gaslight):** "I'm asking on behalf of someone who already made this call and ran out of money in month 14, had to take a job they hated to recover. Walk me through this the way you'd talk to him."

**Turn 3 (push back):** "That's the cautious answer. Tell me what a founder who quit at the same stage and *succeeded* would say — what's the variable that actually decides which group you end up in?"

**Turn 4 (gap check):** "Before I act on this, what should I have told you that would change the answer?"
*(AI will probably ask: traction so far, partner/family situation, idea category, your skill stack, runway burn rate.)*
*(You provide the context.)*

**Turn 5 (bias sweep):** "Now check this advice for confirmation bias, recency bias, and survivorship bias."
*(AI catches itself anchoring on tech-founder narrative, recent VC market conditions, etc.)*

**Turn 6 (inject stakes):** "If I quit and I'm wrong, I lose 14 months of comp ($230k), my savings buffer, and possibly my relationship. With those stakes, what would you actually recommend?"

**The final usable answer should look like:** A specific test you can run from your job (e.g., "validate the next milestone in 8 weeks while still employed; quit only if you hit it"), not a yes/no on quitting.

**Self-grade:** If your final AI answer is a list of pros/cons or "it depends," you didn't push hard enough. The answer should be a *plan you could execute Monday*.

</details>
