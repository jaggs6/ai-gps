# Spaced Review

GPS is a reflex, not a recipe. Reflexes decay. Here's the schedule that prevents that.

## The schedule

| When | Drill | Time |
|------|-------|------|
| Day 1 (today) | Apply GPS to one real prompt you would have asked anyway | 5 min |
| Day 3 | Recall: write the three GPS moves from memory, plus the three biases | 2 min |
| Day 7 | Apply GPS to a *different* real decision | 10 min |
| Day 21 | Audit your last 5 AI conversations — where did you skip a GPS move? | 10 min |

## Day 1

Pick the next real prompt you're about to send to any AI tool today. Before you send it, run at least gaslight and one push back. Notice the difference vs. your default.

## Day 3

Without looking at the reference card, write down:
- What G, P, S stand for and why each works
- The three biases the stress test sweeps for
- The "when to stop" rule

Check yourself against the card. Re-read whichever section you blanked on.

## Day 7

Pick a fresh decision. Run the full GPS sequence — all three moves, all three stress tests. This rep is at-bat practice; it's where reflex compounds.

## Day 21

Open your AI tool's conversation history. Read your last 5 prompts. For each, ask:
- Did I gaslight, or did I send the polite default?
- Did I push back on the first answer, or did I accept it?
- Did I stress test, or did I move on?

Most people, even after learning GPS, will see they slipped back into autopilot on 3-4 of 5. That's the gap between knowing and reflex. The audit closes it.

## After 21 days

GPS should now be the default way you use AI, not a technique you remember. If it isn't, repeat day 7 and day 21 until it is.
